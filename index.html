Essential Equipment

  • Bamboo Rolling Mat (Makisu) - For rolling maki
  • Sharp Knife - Japanese chef's knife or very sharp blade
  • Rice Cooker or Pot - For perfect sushi rice
  • Wooden Spoon (Shamoji) - For mixing rice
  • Bowl of Water - With vinegar to keep hands moist

Sushi Rice Recipe (Serves 4-6)

  1. 2 cups Japanese short-grain rice
  2. 2¬Ω cups water
  3. ‚Öì cup rice vinegar
  4. 2 tablespoons sugar
  5. 1 teaspoon salt

Steps:

  1. Rinse rice 3-4 times until water runs clear
  2. Cook rice with water in rice cooker or pot
  3. Mix vinegar, sugar, and salt until dissolved
  4. Spread cooked rice on wide surface
  5. Pour vinegar mixture over rice while fanning
  6. Gently fold rice to coat evenly
  7. Cool to room temperature before using

üçô Nigiri Sushi

How to Make Nigiri

Nigiri is hand-pressed sushi with a slice of fish over rice.

  1. Wet your hands with water-vinegar mixture
  2. Take about 1 tablespoon of sushi rice
  3. Form an oval shape by gently pressing (not squeezing)
  4. Dab tiny amount of wasabi on fish slice
  5. Place fish slice over rice
  6. Gently press fish onto rice with two fingers
  7. Shape the sides to create neat edges
Pro Tip: The rice should hold together but not be compressed too much - aim for a light, airy texture.

üåØ Maki (Rolled Sushi)

Classic Maki Roll Instructions

Traditional nori-outside rolls with filling inside.

  1. Place bamboo mat on clean surface, cover with plastic wrap
  2. Put nori sheet shiny side down on mat
  3. Wet hands and spread thin layer of rice on nori (leave 1 inch at top)
  4. Create small groove in center of rice
  5. Add your fillings horizontally in the groove
  6. Lift bottom of mat and roll over filling
  7. Press gently to seal, then continue rolling
  8. Seal the top edge with water
  9. Cut into 6-8 pieces with wet, sharp knife

üîÑ Inside-Out Rolls (Uramaki)

How to Make Inside-Out Rolls

Rice on the outside, nori on the inside - perfect for sesame seed coating!

  1. Cover bamboo mat completely with plastic wrap
  2. Place nori sheet on mat
  3. Cover entire nori sheet with even layer of rice
  4. Sprinkle sesame seeds on rice (optional)
  5. Flip entire thing over so nori is facing up
  6. Add fillings in line across center
  7. Roll tightly using mat, rice will be on outside
  8. Press firmly to shape
  9. Cut into 8 pieces with very wet knife

üêâ Dragon Roll: Shrimp tempura, topped with avocado and eel sauce

üéâ Temaki (Hand Rolls)

How to Make Hand Rolls

Fun and easy cone-shaped sushi perfect for parties!

  1. Cut nori sheet in half
  2. Place nori in palm of your hand
  3. Put small amount of rice on left side
  4. Add your favorite fillings diagonally
  5. Roll from bottom left to create cone shape
  6. Seal edge with grain of rice
  7. Eat immediately for best crunch!
Party Tip: Set up a temaki bar with various fillings and let guests make their own!

üí° Expert Tips for Perfect Sushi

üî™ Cutting Tips

Always use a very sharp knife and wet it between each cut. Clean knife with damp towel frequently.

üçö Rice Temperature

Use room temperature rice. Cold rice doesn't stick well, hot rice makes nori soggy.

üíß Keep Hands Wet

Always keep a bowl of water with vinegar nearby to prevent rice from sticking to your hands.

üéØ Don't Overfill

Less is more! Overfilled rolls are difficult to roll and fall apart when cut.

üêü Fresh Fish Only

Always use sushi-grade fish from reputable suppliers. Freeze fish at -20¬∞C for 7 days to kill parasites.

‚è±Ô∏è Practice Timing

Make sushi close to serving time. Nori loses crispness and rice texture changes if prepared too early.
